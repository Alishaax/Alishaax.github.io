What ia Science?

Science is both a body of knowledge and a process. In school, science
may sometimes seem like a collection of isolated and static facts listed
in a textbook, but that’s only a small part of the story. Just as importantly,
science is also a process of discovery that allows us to link isolated facts
into coherent and comprehensive understandings of the natural world.

About Our Department

Rosedale River Secondary School has an outstanding science program.
Students have an opportunity to choose from a variety of courses that
not only focus on teaching big ideas and core content knowledge, but
also helping students to develop 21st Century Learning Skills thus
contributing to students’ advancement as critical thinkers. Students
have opportunities to learn using many different tools and strategies, as
well as ability to share their learning with others in a myriad of ways.

Teaching and learning

Rosedal Rivers Science teachers create teaching and learning activities
designed to meet the individual needs and learning styles of each student.
Many courses use project-based and inquiry-based learning approaches to
ensure all students are engaged and empowered to learn and succeed.

What do we Expect?

Rosedale River Secondary School is full of people who like to learn, are
inherently curious and are open to new ideas. Rosedale Rivers students
can responsibly work on self-directed projects and balance the heavy course
load of the Science School program. They are also committed to principles
of diversity, equity, inclusion and anti-racism.
